Armenia in one place

Armenia is a small country, but it has a history of millennia: here are everything from Yeghegis to the Ararat plain, from the Urartian fortress of Erebuni to modern Yerevan, founded in 782 BC.

In 301 AD Armenia is the first to officially adopt Christianity, and in the 7th century the churches of Hripsime and Gayane are built, shaping the image of early Armenian church architecture.

Today Yerevan brings together old neighbourhoods, Soviet‑era buildings and new cafés and cultural spaces, creating a city where history, everyday life and leisure meet.

Short timeline

  • 301 AD · Armenia becomes the first country to officially adopt Christianity.
  • 405 AD · Mesrop Mashtots creates the Armenian alphabet.
  • 7th century AD · The churches of Hripsime and Gayane are built.
  • 20th century AD · Yerevan becomes a modern capital with both old and new layers.
