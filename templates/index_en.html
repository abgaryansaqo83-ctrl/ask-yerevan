Armenia in one place

Armenia is a small country with a long history: from mountain valleys and the Ararat plain to the Urartian fortress of Erebuni and modern Yerevan, founded in 782 BC.

In 301 AD Armenia became the first country to adopt Christianity as a state religion, and in the 7th century the churches of Hripsime and Gayane were built, shaping early Armenian church architecture.

Today Yerevan brings together old quarters, Soviet‑era buildings and new cafés and cultural spaces, creating a city where history, everyday life and leisure meet.

Short timeline

  • 301 AD · Armenia becomes the first state to adopt Christianity.
  • 405 AD · Mesrop Mashtots creates the Armenian alphabet.
  • 7th century · The churches of Hripsime and Gayane are built near Etchmiadzin.
  • 20th century · Yerevan grows into a modern capital, mixing old and new layers.
